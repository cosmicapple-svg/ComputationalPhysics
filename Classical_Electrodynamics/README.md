# Classical Electrodynamics

A comprehensive collection of numerical simulations in Classical Electrodynamics, implemented in Python.
This module is part of the broader Computational Physics repository and focuses on solving electrodynamics systems that lack closed-form analytical solutions.

---

## Project Overview
Many electrodynamic systems, particularly those involving complex boundary conditions or continuous charge distributions, require numerical techniques to find potentials and fields. 
This project explores how numerical methods can be used to approximate solutions to electrostatic and magnetostatic systems with high accuracy.

The simulations emphasize:

* Physical intuition of field lines and equipotential surfaces
* Numerical stability, convergence properties, and error analysis of relaxation methods
* Computational mapping of potentials and fields from boundary value problems

All models are implemented using modular, self-contained code, allowing each system to be studied independently.

## Topics Covered

### Electrostatics and Field Mapping
* **Electrostatic Fields of Point Charges:** Simulates electric field lines and equipotential surfaces for discrete point charge configurations using Coulomb's Law. It visualizes the nature of monopoles acting as sources/sinks, the coupling of dipoles, and the stable/unstable equilibrium points of quadrupoles.
* **Charged Ring:** Models a continuous distribution of charge by discretizing the path of a ring into a finite number of tightly packed point charges.

### Boundary Value Problems (Laplace's Equation)
* **Square Box:** Solves Laplace's equation ($\vec{\nabla}^2V = 0$) inside an empty square box subject to specific linear boundary conditions. It also computes the resulting constant electric field via numerical derivation.
* **Empty Prism with Metallic Walls and Bar:** Computes the potential distribution between a grounded outer square prism (0V) and an inner metallic bar at a fixed voltage (1V).
* **Capacitor of Parallel Plates:** Simulates the potential and field lines generated by two parallel plates with opposite charges, showing edge effects where field lines extend to infinity.

### Charge Distributions (Poisson's Equation)
* **Point Charge Potential:** Solves Poisson's equation ($\vec{\nabla}^2V = -\frac{\rho}{\epsilon_0}$) to compute the potential in a region containing a localized point charge. It demonstrates the sharp peak in potential at the charge's location and the concentric circular equipotential paths.

### Magnetostatics
* **Magnetic Dipole:** Uses a discretized application of the Biot-Savart Law to model the magnetic field generated by steady currents or moving charges. It visualizes the fundamental lack of magnetic monopoles by illustrating how magnetic field lines form closed continuous loops around their sources.

## Numerical Methods

Because partial differential equations (PDEs) like Laplace's and Poisson's cannot be solved with standard Runge-Kutta methods, the `simulation_electro.py` script utilizes the following numerical techniques:
* **Finite Differences:** Used to discretize continuous field lines and to compute numerical spatial derivatives (gradients) to derive electric fields from scalar potentials.
* **Jacobi Method:** An iterative relaxation algorithm where the potential at any grid point is calculated as the average of its adjacent neighboring points.
* **Gauss-Seidel Method:** An improved relaxation method that utilizes newly computed values within the current iteration step, effectively reducing the number of required iterations for convergence by a factor of 2.
* **Successive Over-Relaxation (SOR):** A highly optimized relaxation method that applies an over-relaxation factor $\alpha$ to the changes recommended by the Gauss-Seidel method, dramatically accelerating convergence.
